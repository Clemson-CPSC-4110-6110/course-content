What is Virtual Reality?

What is Virtual Reality?

  • Virtual Reality (VR) replaces the real world with artificially created sensory input
  • Augmented Reality (AR) augments the real world with artificially created sensory input
  • Current technology works best for visual and auditory information
  • Research extends to: touch, taste, smell, and vestibular sense

VR Beyond Humans

VR isn't just for humans - researchers have created VR systems for mice, flies, and other organisms to study behavior and perception.

Defining VR

Five important components:

  1. Organism — Immersive VR only happens if a creature perceives it
  2. Targeted behavior — Organism experiences something as designed
  3. Artificial sensory stimulation — Experience created through artificial sensory information
  4. Awareness — The organism is "fooled" into feeling present
  5. Interaction — The user not only perceives but also acts

Human-Centered Computing and VR

  • Human Centered Computing (HCC) recognizes the intersection between technology, environment, and person
  • Every system you design is intended to be used by a person in a context
  • We can design better systems when we remember this
  • HCC perspective is especially important for VR — the person-system coupling is much tighter

How Do We Learn?

  • We learn by effortfully applying ourselves to difficult things
  • Significant learning occurs through failure, refinement, and repetition
  • Excellence requires striving past your current ability
  • Setbacks provide information needed to achieve mastery

When learning is easy, it is often superficial and quickly forgotten

The Science of Successful Learning

Key Principles

  • Easy learning isn't durable
  • Regular retrieval strengthens memories
  • Interleaving problems is more effective

Effective Strategies

  • Elaboration — Finding additional meaning
  • Generation — Attempt before receiving solutions
  • Reflection — Connect to other concepts
  • Calibration — Objectively evaluate knowledge
  • Mnemonics — Mental structures for retrieval

Physical Writing vs Typing

Research shows physically writing notes leads to more durable memories

  • Can't copy verbatim when writing — requires synthesis
  • Writing requires intricate motor skills, engages more brain regions
  • Motor and vision regions synchronize with memory regions
  • Similar benefits for paper or tablet with pen

Specification Grading

What is Specification Grading?

  • Grading system focused on clear, specific criteria
  • Assignments graded as Satisfactory or Unsatisfactory
  • Projects and exams divided into bundles
  • Each bundle must be fully completed to earn points
  • No partial credit within a bundle
  • Unsatisfactory work can be resubmitted after feedback
