Welcome!

Our course Discord server is a space for you to connect with classmates, get help with technical issues, collaborate with your team, and engage with course material outside of class time.

Discord is optional but highly recommended. Many students find it valuable for getting quick help from peers‚Äîoften faster than waiting for office hours or email responses.

Getting Started

Step 1: Create a Discord Account

If you don't already have Discord:

  1. Go to discord.com or download the Discord app (desktop/mobile)
  2. Click "Register" and create an account
  3. Verify your email address

Username tip: Use something recognizable (your name or Clemson username) so classmates and the instructor can identify you.

Step 2: Join the Server

  1. Click the invite link posted on Canvas (Announcements or Syllabus page)
  2. Accept the invitation
  3. Read the rules in #rules and react to confirm you've read them
  4. Introduce yourself in #general (name, year, one thing you're excited to build in VR)

Getting the Most Out of Discord

Getting Help

  • Search first: Use Ctrl/Cmd+F to search channels. Your error message has probably been asked about before.
  • Be specific: "My build is failing" isn't helpful. Include the error message, what you tried, and screenshots if possible.
  • Use code blocks: Wrap code or error messages in backticks (`) for single line or triple backticks (```) for multiple lines.
  • Pay it forward: If you solve a problem, share the solution. Help someone else when you can.

Team Communication

  • Use your team channel: Keep project discussion centralized rather than scattered across DMs.
  • Post async updates: Quick messages about what you worked on help the team stay aligned.
  • Voice for complex topics: Some things are easier to talk through than type. Jump in the voice channel.
  • Pin important info: Right-click ‚Üí Pin Message to save key decisions or links.

Office Hours

The instructor will host drop-in office hours in the "Office Hours" voice channel during scheduled times. You can:

  • Join the channel and wait (you'll be helped in order)
  • Screen share to show your Unity project
  • Listen while others get help (you might learn something)

Expectations

Community Standards

  • Be respectful and professional
  • No harassment, discrimination, or inappropriate content
  • Academic integrity applies here‚Äîdon't share exam answers or code that should be individual work
  • Keep discussions constructive

Response Times

  • From classmates: Often fast! The community is helpful.
  • From instructor/TA: We check Discord but not 24/7. For urgent grading issues, use email.
  • Don't expect instant answers: If it's truly urgent and no one responds, come to office hours.
